# Voice Samples — The Narrator

> Sample flavor text in the unified anonymous "we" narrator voice. Written to pressure-test the voice across the full arc of the game and to give us a reference to maintain consistency over the full 100–150 lines.

The narrator never identifies themselves. They are durable enough to have watched from before the first stars and to be present at the last. They are not a god. They grieve. They speak in first-person plural — *we* — until the very end.

## Long-burn voice considerations (2026-05-11)

The long-burn pacing shift changes how the narrator's forward-pointing language lands in player time.

- **Forward-pointing lines now point across real time (days, not minutes).** When the T5 Galaxy tier-up says *"We can feel the next gravity already,"* the next gravity is days away in active play, weeks in idle play. The narrator's voice gets the **temporal distance it was always written for**.
- **The peak (T6 Local Group) lands days into the play.** Engaged Completion calendar target ~5-7 days; the peak line is the most polished line in the game, partly because it has to carry awe across a relationship that has lasted real days.
- **The Eridanus Reach pivot lands days after the peak.** T7 Galactic Cluster Engaged Completion calendar is ~4-6 days after the peak. The narrator confessing *"We had not been counting"* lands a long time after the player stopped paying attention to the hidden number — the not-counting itself is real-time-scale.
- **Idle interjections (CD-5).** The narrator does not perform to an empty room. Accumulated narrator events are held until return; on return, the narrator greets the player with a quieter line, never the loudest. The narrator's voice is calibrated to a present player, not to a queue of missed beats.
- **Heat death is asymptotic (SD-5).** The late voice implies approach, not arrival. *"Goodnight"* is the narrator's act, not the universe's; the universe continues, infinitely slowly, after the screen fades.

## Cosmological time-arc note (SD-3, SD-4, SD-5)

For T7+ writing (post-peak — Act 2), the narrator's voice is grounded in real cosmological epochs (see design-notes §16.5). Three constraints:

- **Event horizon, not particle horizon (SD-3).** "Causal Horizon" (the final tier — T11 under the 2026-05-13 11-tier renumber) and "causal threshold" (Eridanus Reach pivot) refer specifically to the cosmological **event horizon** — what we can ever reach — not the particle horizon (what we've seen). In a Λ-dominated universe these diverge; the design specifically commits to event horizon as the player's true horizon. Avoid prose that conflates the two.
- **CMB cools monotonically (SD-4).** The through-line registers a monotonically decreasing temperature across the arc. The hum cools; the light fades; the photon energy density falls below detectability by the final tier (Causal Horizon, T11). **CMB temperature only goes down, never bounces.** A line like *"the microwave hum has cooled by a fraction"* is on-line; a line that suggests warming or rebound is not.
- **Heat death is asymptotic (SD-5).** The late voice implies approach, not arrival. Avoid prose that frames heat death as an event ("when heat death arrives," "at the heat death"). Prefer *approach* language ("the hum fades," "the last warmth thins," "what remains continues, infinitely slowly").

## Idle interjections — fill the space between

These play at internally scheduled intervals during quiet stretches. They have no mechanical trigger; they are the narrator's voice in the dead air.

### Act 1 — wonder, slow
> A planet forms in our reach today. We will not see who walks on it.

> The cosmic microwave background hums. It has hummed for thirteen billion years. It is the oldest light there is, and it is everywhere, and it is fading.

> Somewhere in our gravity, a star is being born. Somewhere else, an older one is dying. The numbers, on average, are equal. They will not always be.

### transitional — first quiet doubt
> We have been gathering for a long time. We have only recently begun to wonder what the gathering is for.

### Act 2 — grief, specific
> There was a galaxy here once. We are no longer certain of its name.

> Andromeda is still with us. We mention this because it has not always seemed certain.

> Light from a structure that no longer reaches us arrives today. It has been traveling since before there were stars. We receive it. We have nothing to send back.

### late Act 2 — the narrator sounds tired
> We do not know how long we have been doing this.

> The smaller the universe becomes, the heavier each thing in it.

> Once, there were so many of us that we did not know each other's names. Now we know all of them.

## What the voice is doing — the rules behind the lines

For when we scale this up to 100+ lines and need consistency:

- **First-person plural throughout, until the very end.** *We, us, our.* No exceptions until the final monologue.
- **Present tense, even for deep time.** The narrator is always *here, now*, watching.
- **Numbers are weapons in Act 2, decorations in Act 1.** *"Twelve solar masses"* in Act 1 is a flourish. *"6.5 billion solar masses, drifting"* in Act 2 is an obituary.
- **Specific names whenever possible.** *Andromeda, Eridanus, NGC 1300, M87, Boötes.* Real things. Real losses.
- **Sentences shorten as the game descends.** Compare Tier 6 (the peak) to Tier 11 (Causal Horizon). The narrator runs out of breath.
- **No exclamation points. Ever.** The narrator is too old for exclamation points.
- **No second person.** The narrator never says *you*. The player is part of the *we* or they are not addressed at all.
- **No metaphors that aren't physics.** The narrator describes things as they are. The grief is in the description, not in the figurative language.
- **Real cosmological vocabulary only.** *Causal threshold. Gravitational reach. Solar masses. Cosmic horizon.* No invented terms.
- **The narrator confesses, never accuses.** *"We had not been counting"* — the narrator is implicated, not the player. Player feels guilty by inclusion in the *we*, never by direct address.
- **Verb-agency check.** When the narrator says *we*, the verb decides whether *we* reads as a structure (consequence of gravity) or as actors (a civilization with intent). If the sentence works with physics in the subject slot — *"the pull deepens"*, *"what was scattered keeps the same beat"* — prefer that. Reserve transitive acquisitive verbs (*pull, keep, catch, hold, take*) with *we* as subject for moments where the structural reading is intended (the late-tier collapse into *"we are the largest thing we have ever been"*). Brown Dwarf is the reference: *"We feel it in the orbits of the things we can see"* — the action is in the orbits; *we* is the consequence.

## Authoring strategy for the full text

Now that the voice is locked, the 100–150 lines fall into batches that can be written sequentially:

1. **Tier-up lines** (~10) — the most polished. These are the spine. Write these first.
2. **The peak line** (Local Group, Tier 6 under the 2026-05-13 11-tier renumber; was Tier 5) — the single most important line. Polish indefinitely.
3. **The pivot — Eridanus Reach** — the second most important line. Polish indefinitely.
4. **Named-connection breaks** (~30–50) — Act 2's heartbeat. Each one names something specific that is being lost. These can be written in batches by mood: early-Act-2 (precise), mid-Act-2 (mournful), late-Act-2 (depleted).
5. **Idle interjections** (~30–50) — the connective tissue. Smaller, quieter, more frequent. These can be authored last because they're the most flexible — they fill in gaps once the structural lines are set.
6. **Upgrade descriptions** (~30–50) — same voice, applied to mechanics. The hardest to keep consistent because mechanics tempt straightforward language. Write these last, when the voice is fully internalized.
7. **Endgame** (~5–10) — the singular voice, the goodnight. Most of these already drafted above.

Write in this order. Voice consistency is much easier when the most important lines exist first and the rest are calibrated against them.
